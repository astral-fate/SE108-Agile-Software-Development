Course Objectives

  • Explain the objectives and principles of Agile Software Development.
  • Compare and contrast Agile methods with traditional methods like Waterfall.
  • Describe various Agile methodologies, including XP, Scrum, DSDM, and FDD.
  • Understand the roles, ceremonies, and artifacts within the Scrum framework.
  • Explain the process steps and key techniques used in DSDM and FDD.
  • Understand the fundamental concepts and importance of Software Testing.
  • Differentiate between Verification and Validation and their respective methods.
  • Explain different software testing methods (Black-Box, White-Box, Grey-Box).
  • Describe various software testing levels and specific types (Unit, Integration, System, Regression, Alpha, Beta, Non-functional).
  • Understand Agile Effort Estimation techniques, including Story Points and Velocity.
  • Explain the factors that influence team velocity in Agile projects.

Grading

  • 60%: Attendance, Assignments, Quizzes, Midterm Exam
  • 40%: Final Exam

Assignments (Based on Source Material)

  • **Assignment 1 (Unit 3):** Describe in detail the FDD Method in your project in terms of: Stage 1 (Develop overall project), Stage 2 (Build the Feature List), and Stage 3 (Plan by feature).
  • **Potential Assignment (Unit 4):** Develop a test plan for a given system (e.g., a student table or car sales business as discussed in slides), including identifying validation/verification methods for specific fields.
  • **Potential Assignment (Unit 5):** Calculate the estimated size for a set of user stories given their Story Points, Implementation Level Factor, and Complexity using the Story Size formula.
